# P0–P3 Kill Criteria: Horizon-Sufficiency Project

**Status:** frozen P0 contract  
**Drafted:** 2026-07-16  
**Frozen:** 2026-07-16 after independent claim, mathematics, and collision red-teams  
**Current state:** `HOLD`  
**Default on ambiguity:** do not advance  
**Budget rule:** no method implementation, checkpoint rental, or training is
authorized merely by the existence of this document

These criteria are designed to prevent a failed coordinate-invariance idea from
being repeatedly renamed and rescued. A `KILL` is permanent for the strong AAAI
method version. A `DOWNGRADE` may preserve a diagnostic or workshop result, but
it does not authorize continued optimizer development.

## 1. Decision states

| State | Meaning | Permitted action |
|---|---|---|
| `KILL` | The strong-method thesis is false, collided, or operationally trivial | Archive the method direction; retain only reproducibility evidence |
| `DOWNGRADE` | A valid diagnostic remains, but no strong method contribution survives | Write an analysis/benchmark plan only; no optimizer training |
| `HOLD` | A narrow claim remains plausible but a required gate is missing | Run only the next frozen, cheapest discriminating gate |
| `GO-P1` | Claim and math survive P0 | Freeze and run the natural-update phenomenon gate |
| `GO-P2` | A clean held-out Horizon Gap survives P1 | Develop one certificate/estimator against matched-compute baselines |
| `GO-P3` | The estimator adds operational value | Run cross-architecture and parameter-training validation |

The current project is `HOLD`, not `GO-P1`, until the claim contract, C2
compatibility decision, and a new phenomenon preregistration are frozen.

## 2. Permanent decisions inherited from earlier work

The following may not be reopened by renaming them.

1. The nearest-neighbor coordinate-invariance phenomenon is stopped after its
   two frozen failures.
2. “No Privileged Coordinates” is not an available headline after the 2026 gauge
   and representation-geometry literature.
3. Euclidean-versus-Fisher activation geometry is not an available method claim
   after softmax information geometry and FishBack.
4. A coordinate-equivariant `F^{-1}g` update is not an available optimization
   principle after natural-gradient and trust-region work.
5. The Coconut C1c result is an engineering transport contract, not evidence for
   a natural Horizon Gap.
6. The delimiter-confounded GPT-2 BCG pilot is not positive evidence. Its H1
   effect is substantially explained by the fixed `###` prefix; H2/H3 remain
   mandatory baselines.

Any paper narrative that depends on reversing one of these decisions is `KILL`.

## 3. P0 claim and mathematics gates

The strong-method project is immediately `KILL` if any item below is true.

### P0-K1: claim subtraction leaves only a known optimizer

After subtracting FishBack, path Fisher, natural gradients, TRPO, and recent
latent-dynamics work, the proposal is only “compute `F_H^{-1}g` for a latent
state,” including pseudoinverse, damping, or ordinary low-rank variants.

### P0-K2: no new certificate or estimator target

The only theory consists of standard Fisher decomposition, horizon monotonicity,
coordinate equivariance, generalized eigenvalues, or a generic contraction tail
bound. There is no finite-sample, compute-aware decision that can certify when a
short horizon is sufficient.

The method is also `KILL` or `DOWNGRADE` if it is only a Certified-World-Model-
style adapted-metric/Lyapunov predictability-horizon certificate with trajectory
Fisher substituted as the metric, without a new simultaneous guarantee over
stochastic reference histories and adaptive rollout counts plus a matched-compute
advantage for natural-update risk decisions.

### P0-K3: invalid trajectory object

The proposed target silently replaces the reference-history expectation by a
single factual, greedy, teacher-forced, or independently generated path and
still calls the result the population continuation Fisher.

### P0-K4: ill-posed geometry

The update is driven by Fisher null-space components, chart-specific `lambda I`,
independently regenerated Euclidean subspaces, or incorrect covector/vector
transport, and realized finite-step KL does not obey the claimed trust region.

### P0-K5: activation-to-training leap

The method is presented as a training optimizer even though the only defined
object is a single-interface activation Fisher and the parameter-space pullback
conditions do not hold or are not measured.

### P0-K6: collision appears before execution

A contemporaneous paper already supplies recursive-latent multi-horizon
continuation risk together with an adaptive horizon certificate of comparable
scope. Re-audit is mandatory before P1, P2, and submission.

### P0-K7: local proxy is sold as finite-step behavior

The certificate controls only `0.5 v^T(F_H + lambda M)v`, but the title or theorem
claims a guarantee for actual finite-step sequence KL without a uniform Taylor
remainder bound or an independently calibrated expected-KL upper-confidence line
search.

### P0-K8: regularization manufactures sufficiency

The short horizon passes only after increasing `lambda M`, the regularizer
dominates the accepted directions, or `lambda` is selected by certificate pass
rate rather than an independent numerical/optimization rule.

### P0-K9: optional stopping or unseen-tail guarantee is invalid

Coverage holds only at a fixed sample count, not simultaneously over adaptive
horizons and rollout counts; or the unseen tail is extrapolated from test-prefix
decay or average spectral radius without a structural or independently calibrated
upper envelope.

### P0-K10: the wrong stochastic Fisher is reported

A joint/complete-data Fisher conditioned on sampled internal latent noise is
reported as the observable marginal-output Fisher, or a greedy C2 path is used to
skip the stochastic reference-history P1 gate.

Passing P0 authorizes only a frozen P1 protocol. It does not authorize training.

## 4. SWITCH C2 compatibility boundary

The frozen SWITCH C2 experiment must remain unchanged and interpretable under
its original decision rule. It uses the unperturbed greedy visible history for
teacher forcing. Its `V1/V3/V8/V32/V64` matrices are path-conditioned prefix
geometry, not an expectation over stochastic reference histories.

Therefore:

1. C2 can be a fixed greedy-prefix architecture screen or engineering
   measurement.
2. A C2 pass cannot by itself establish the revised population Horizon-Gap
   claim in `P0_MATHEMATICAL_SPEC.md`.
3. The original conjunctive coordinate verdict cannot be rewritten after seeing
   results.
4. Reuse of C2 code or raw outputs requires a separately frozen compatibility
   overlay that labels path-conditioned versus expected quantities.
5. If a stochastic multi-rollout overlay cannot be added without changing the
   scientific population, eligibility, or held-out partition, run a new P1
   experiment instead of post-hoc relabeling C2.
6. Renting a 96 GB GPU solely to run C2 is not authorized until the compatibility
   audit shows which revised claim, if any, the result can decide.

## 5. P1 natural-update phenomenon gate

P1 must be frozen before any held-out checkpoint result. Its primary result must
hold for the stochastic reference-history expectation, not only a greedy or
factual path. It must compare a longer observable continuation target against
all short and cheap alternatives on natural update families.

### 5.1 Required population

At least two of the following update families must be primary, and one must be
an objective- or optimizer-derived update:

- factual or reward objective gradients;
- exploration perturbations produced by the actual algorithm;
- projection, bridge, or latent-policy head updates;
- adapter/LoRA parameter updates mapped through the complete trajectory; or
- real optimizer steps from a frozen training replay.

Random stress directions may supplement this population but cannot replace it.
Artificial coordinate transformations are metamorphic controls only.
Each family must pass calibration-frozen representation-support and behavioral
OOD checks. “Derived from a task gradient” does not automatically mean natural.

### 5.2 Required baselines

The best calibration-selected member of every applicable family must be present:

- `H1`, `H2`, `H3`, `H8`, and a semantic-boundary prefix;
- one-step FishBack-style pullback Fisher;
- activation whitening or another calibration-only canonical metric;
- exact conditional-KL sums for a fixed path and a high-precision Monte
  Carlo/UCB expected-KL line search for the stochastic target;
- reduced step size / norm matching;
- direct reference-rollout Monte Carlo continuation KL at matched rollout and
  wall-clock budget;
- recurrent-Jacobian spectral-radius or STARS-style stability diagnostics;
- a Certified-World-Model-style adapted-metric/predictability-horizon baseline;
- representation-support and intervention-OOD diagnostics; and
- the long-horizon oracle, which cannot be selected as a deployable method.

### 5.3 Binding phenomenon thresholds

The exact estimand and hierarchical bootstrap unit must be frozen in the P1
preregistration. The short horizon `h`, certificate horizon `H_cert`, and
seed-disjoint behavioral endpoint `H_eval` must be distinct labels. Calibration
must verify that `H_cert`, `2 H_cert`, or semantic completion gives a stable
oracle ranking. At minimum, the long-horizon signal must, on disjoint test
prompts:

1. improve strict-tail risk Spearman correlation over the best simple baseline
   by at least `0.10`, with a prompt-clustered 95% lower confidence bound of at
   least `0.03`;
2. improve top-risk recall by at least `0.10`;
3. reduce false-safe decisions by a preregistered practically meaningful amount
   without making almost every update unsafe; the preregistration must give a
   minimum absolute count, prompt/problem coverage, prevalence, and
   practical-equivalence margin;
4. produce an expected reference-rollout long-horizon KL ratio no greater than
   `0.90` for a matched-utility or matched-gain update, with the 95% upper bound
   below `1.0`;
5. preserve held-out factual utility or reward within a frozen non-inferiority
   margin; and
6. remain present under group splits or hierarchical clustering for every shared
   prompt, template, checkpoint, and optimizer trajectory, with simultaneous
   correction across horizons, models, and update families; and
7. remain present when Fisher construction histories are seed-disjoint from
   finite-step KL and ranking-label histories.

Thresholds 4–5 are a fixed-long-horizon oracle witness that the phenomenon is
operationally material. They are not C3a. C3a later requires the adaptive
certificate to recover that advantage under matched compute without routinely
paying the oracle cost.

These are minimum effect requirements inherited where compatible from SWITCH
C2. The P1 preregistration may strengthen them but may not weaken them after
observing test outcomes.

### 5.4 P1 permanent kill conditions

Set the strong-method project to `KILL` if any one occurs:

1. Correct reference-history expectation removes the apparent Horizon Gap.
2. The effect exists only at a formatting delimiter, EOS artifact, fixed factual
   path, one prompt template, or adversarial chart.
3. `H2/H3/H8`, a semantic prefix, whitening, smaller steps, or exact scalar
   short-KL retuning recovers the long-horizon decision within the frozen
   practical-equivalence margin.
4. Direct Monte Carlo continuation KL is as accurate and no more expensive than
   the proposed estimation target.
5. The effect disappears for objective/optimizer-derived updates and survives
   only for searched worst-case directions.
6. A recurrent-Jacobian spectral diagnostic explains the same decisions at
   lower cost, so the result reduces to generic instability.
7. The point estimate passes only after test-dependent choice of horizon,
   subspace, damping, threshold, prompt subset, or rollout seeds.
8. The effect lacks the minimum rank, recall, expected-KL, or utility margins.
9. The long-horizon oracle is not stable at `2 H_cert` or semantic completion.
10. Representation-support/OOD controls show that delayed drift is a dormant-path
    artifact of off-support activation intervention.
11. Seed-disjoint history cross-fitting removes the correlation or false-safe
    advantage.
12. The strong claim holds in fewer than two natural update families.
13. A joint/internal-noise Fisher, rather than the declared observable marginal
    Fisher, drives the result.

If the phenomenon is valid but too small for a method, use `DOWNGRADE`, not a
weaker post-hoc gate.

## 6. P2 adaptive-certificate gate

P2 is authorized only after P1 passes. Only one primary estimator/certificate
family may be selected from calibration evidence before the main test.

The method must:

1. stop adaptively without first computing the full oracle horizon;
2. provide simultaneous projected-Loewner bounds over both inspected horizons
   and adaptive rollout counts, not plug-in spectral estimates;
3. account for optional stopping, shared histories, estimation of the short-risk
   matrix, and estimator bias;
4. preserve P1 update rankings and false-safe decisions;
5. beat direct Monte Carlo continuation KL, fixed `H8`, and fixed long-horizon
   Fisher under matched wall time, rollout calls, and peak memory; and
6. deliver operational value, not merely approximate a matrix in Frobenius norm;
7. freeze damping independently and report raw tail, regularized tail,
   regularizer share, and an `M`-only baseline; and
8. use a structural or independently calibrated envelope for unobserved future
   increments, not test-prefix extrapolation.

Every expected-KL UCB suffix call is charged to the proposed method. If the
finite-step bridge routinely evaluates the same long horizon as direct Monte
Carlo and does not improve the risk/compute frontier, set `KILL` or `DOWNGRADE`.

The theorem is a **local trajectory-Fisher** certificate unless a uniform
remainder bound or expected-KL UCB line search separately bridges it to
finite-step behavior.

Set the strong method to `KILL` if any item below is true:

- the certificate is a renamed geometric-series bound with a test-fitted decay
  constant;
- optional-stopping coverage handles horizons but not adaptive sample count;
- a spectral-radius average is used as a tail bound despite possible non-normal
  transient amplification;
- the certificate controls only local quadratic risk but is marketed as a
  finite-step behavioral guarantee;
- increasing `lambda M` is what makes the certificate pass;
- the method must compute almost the full horizon on nearly every sample;
- direct Monte Carlo matches or dominates its risk/compute frontier;
- the advantage disappears under a seed-disjoint expected-KL UCB line search; or
- the only gain comes from ordinary low-rank Fisher, damping, or better code.

A valid diagnostic with a failed P2 becomes `DOWNGRADE`.

## 7. P3 replication and training gate

A competitive strong-method paper requires both:

1. replication in a second mechanism-distinct recurrent or soft latent-reasoning
   architecture; and
2. a parameter-space bridge plus actual multi-step training, not activation
   steering or replay alone.

The second architecture must not be a checkpoint-only variant of the first.
Tasks must include more than one formatting template and at least one meaningful
distribution shift or reasoning family change.

Set the strong method to `KILL` if:

- the second architecture fails the preregistered phenomenon gate;
- activation risk does not predict parameter-update risk;
- replayed optimizer evidence does not transfer to actual multi-step training;
- training gains disappear under matched tokens, rollouts, wall time, seeds, and
  peak memory;
- utility gains require relaxing the certified risk threshold;
- results depend on one seed or one benchmark family; or
- the full method is dominated by the best simple baseline within the frozen
  practical-equivalence margin.

If P1 and P2 pass but parameter training fails, preserve the work only as a
diagnostic/certificate paper if that remaining package independently meets the
target venue's contribution bar.

## 8. Authorization sequence

The only valid order is:

1. draft the claim contract, mathematical specification, collision matrix, kill
   criteria, and C2 compatibility audit;
2. complete independent claim, mathematics, and collision red-team reviews;
3. resolve every blocking objection and re-review the changed text;
4. freeze the P0 documents and record their date and hashes;
5. freeze an additive C2 overlay or a separate stochastic natural-update P1
   preregistration without changing the old C2 protocol;
6. run the cheapest source/CPU controls;
7. run calibration only;
8. authorize held-out P1 only if controls and calibration remain valid;
9. implement an adaptive method only after a clean P1 pass; and
10. authorize training only after the P2 matched-compute gate passes.

Skipping a stage, weakening a threshold after seeing data, or presenting an
earlier engineering pass as evidence triggers `KILL` for the strong-method
version.
