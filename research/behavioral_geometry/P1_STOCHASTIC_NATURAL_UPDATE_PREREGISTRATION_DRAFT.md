## 0. Red-team resolution and current decision

The three independent reviews agreed that the previous last-action estimand was
not executable as written and was scientifically too close to FishBack plus a
longer ordinary autoregressive path Fisher. The binding v2 decisions are:

1. intervene at the first structurally eligible soft latent action under the
   official deterministic no-noise policy;
2. recompute the entire later latent chain, proxy ids, supports, exit decision,
   and KV state under each candidate rather than clamping the factual suffix;
3. use one common deterministic reference action for every update family;
4. use a full-support temperature-only visible law for the primary smooth KL;
5. retain the official hard top-k/top-p law only as an extended-real deployment
   sensitivity in which support loss is `+infinity`, never epsilon-smoothed;
6. estimate only directional risks for preregistered natural updates, not an
   undefined full Fisher matrix or an arbitrary coordinate subspace;
7. make natural-late, same-vector late, and matched ordinary activation
   interfaces mandatory specificity controls; and
8. treat no-exit, support changes, and discrete trace changes as explicit
   structural endpoints that cannot provide positive Horizon-Gap evidence.

The revised artifact passed independent source, mathematics/statistics, and
collision review at the design level. It does **not** resolve the source adapter,
fake-model validation, tail/trace support, measured compute budget, statistical
power, or final execution-freeze blockers.

## 1. Scientific question, hypothesis, and scope

### 1.1 Question

For updates generated by an actual latent-policy exploration rule or its
declared reward objective, can a cheap short observable horizon materially
underprice delayed continuation risk created by deterministic recursive latent
computation?

### 1.2 Primary hypothesis

On seed-disjoint held-out problems, reference histories, and construction
histories, a fixed long-horizon expected **directional** trajectory Fisher at an
early recursive latent interface will rank post-H8 finite-step continuation KL
materially better than every short, semantic-prefix, support, stability, and
matched-compute direct-Monte-Carlo baseline. The advantage must remain inside a
trace-stable local population, improve false-safe decisions at matched
acceptance coverage, and reduce expected long-horizon KL at non-inferior task
utility.

The early-interface Horizon Gap must also be materially larger than the gap at:

- the last nonterminal soft latent action, where no substantial later latent
  recursion remains; and
- a matched ordinary non-latent hidden-state interface.

Otherwise the result is a generic autoregressive activation diagnostic rather
than evidence specific to recursive latent reasoning.

### 1.3 Nonclaims

P1 does not claim:

- that trajectory Fisher, path-space information, natural gradients, or
  pullback softmax geometry are new;
- that a fixed long-horizon Fisher is the proposed final method;
- that a local quadratic form is automatically a finite-step certificate;
- that the full-support analysis law is the released deployment sampler;
- that a context-specific objective-gradient counterfactual is an optimizer or
  parameter update;
- that discrete support or exit transitions are differentiable;
- that two constructions from one checkpoint establish mechanism generality;
  or
- that the old greedy SWITCH C2 estimates a reference-history expectation.

## 2. Primary recursive estimand

### 2.1 Deterministic reference latent policy

Run the pinned official evaluation policy with
`add_noise_gumbel_softmax=False`. At a latent step with logits `ell`, the source
action is the deterministic top-10 vocabulary mixture

\[
z_{\rm det}(\ell)
=\sum_{i\in\operatorname{Top10}(\ell)}
\operatorname{softmax}(\ell_{\operatorname{Top10}})_i E_i,
\]

and the top-1 id is the latent proxy. Conditional on the request not already
terminating through the source's generic length/token/string stop checks, the
source switches from latent to visible mode only when the proxy equals the
pinned latent-end id `524`.

The primary interface `j*` is the **first** source-declared nonterminal soft
latent action. It is eligible only if the unperturbed deterministic trace:

1. survives the source `check_finished` call at `j*` and has proxy id different
   from `524`;
2. contains at least four later nonterminal soft latent actions before exit;
3. exits naturally by latent step 32; and
4. passes the visible semantic-tail rule in Section 4.3.

No later interface may be searched if the first action fails. The platform is
`NO-GO`; this prevents risk-dependent interface selection. Let `c` be the full
state before `j*` and let

\[
z_0(c)=z_{\rm det}(\ell(c))
\]

be the single common reference action for every family and candidate.

### 2.2 Candidate intervention and recursive closure

The executed source action is a joint object: mixture embedding `z`, proxy id
`p`, the current request/decoder state `sigma`, and the support/weight record
that produced them. Family A supplies its noisy top-1 proxy; Family B supplies
the deterministic top-1 proxy after its objective counterfactual. Before any
candidate is constructed, freeze `ignore_eos`, `max_new_tokens`, every EOS id,
`sampling_params.stop_token_ids`, tokenizer `additional_stop_token_ids`, every
stop string, its decoder convention, and `stop_str_max_len`. For candidate
action `(z_0+v,p_v)`, intervene only at `j*` and use the stateful source execution
map

\[
 \sigma'=\operatorname{CheckFinished}(\operatorname{Append}(\sigma,p)),
 \qquad r(\sigma,p)=\operatorname{FinishedReason}(\sigma'),
 \qquad
\operatorname{Exec}(z,p,\sigma)=
\begin{cases}
\operatorname{STOP}(r),
& r(\sigma,p)\ne\varnothing,\\
(E_{524},\operatorname{VISIBLE}),
& r(\sigma,p)=\varnothing\ \text{and}\ p=524,\\
(z,\operatorname{LATENT}),
& r(\sigma,p)=\varnothing\ \text{and}\ p\ne524.
\end{cases}
\]

This precedence is binding: the scheduler appends `p` and calls
`check_finished()` (which mutates `finished_reason`) before
`update_latent_info()`. A latent-time length, EOS/stop
token, additional-stop token, or stop-string match therefore terminates the
request before either `z` or `E_524` can be consumed by a later model step.
Record these absorbing endpoints respectively as `LATENT_FINISH_LENGTH`,
`LATENT_FINISH_TOKEN`, and `LATENT_FINISH_STRING`; an external abort is an
`EXECUTION_ABORT` hard failure rather than analyzable data. Such endpoints have
no visible continuation and cannot provide positive local-Fisher or Horizon-Gap
evidence. Preflight must verify that id `524` is absent from every frozen stop-id
set. Conditional on no generic stop, the source scheduler overwrites a proposed
soft mixture with one-hot token 524 when that proxy triggers latent exit; such a
mixture is recorded but is not falsely described as consumed by the model.
Thereafter, at every recursively recomputed latent step, apply the same
append--check--execute order and:

- do not teacher-force any reference latent action or proxy;
- recompute all future deterministic top-10 mixtures and proxy ids;
- update the candidate KV/cache state with its own soft actions;
- let the candidate generate its own latent-end transition; and
- stop the latent closure at the natural exit or the frozen cap of 32 latent
  steps.

After each trajectory's own exit, visible token index one is aligned to visible
token index one even if reference and candidate used different latent lengths.
The observable law is therefore a distribution over visible continuations
after a deterministic internal closure, not a distribution conditioned on a
forced common exit.

A candidate that neither terminates nor exits latent mode by the cap is retained
as `LATENT_TIMEOUT`. It is never resampled or deleted and is reported as a
separate deployment failure endpoint. A tangent `q_H(v)` at the reference base
cell may still be computed when the injected proxy is common, but it has no
valid finite-step bridge to a timeout or latent-stop endpoint and cannot enter
the primary trace-stable ranking or count as positive Horizon-Gap evidence.

### 2.3 Piecewise-smooth trace population

The deterministic latent policy contains top-10 selection, top-1 proxies, and a
discrete exit boundary. The local trajectory Fisher is interpretable only in a
stable discrete trace cell.

Before any visible risk label is generated, evaluate each update on the frozen
interpolation grid `alpha in {0, .25, .50, .75, 1}`. An update is
`TRACE-STABLE` only if its injected candidate proxy equals the reference proxy
and, for every grid point after the injected action, using that common proxy
while interpolating the continuous embedding:

- latent closure exits by step 32;
- latent length, top-1 proxy sequence, and exit step equal the reference;
- every subsequent deterministic latent top-10 support equals the reference
  support; and
- the frozen numerical margin checks for the 10th/11th logit and top-1 runner-up
  pass.

The margin tolerance must be selected from dtype and finite-difference error
tests, never from a Horizon-Gap outcome. Grid stability is an operational
diagnostic, not a mathematical proof of stability at every real `alpha`.

Candidate actions with a different injected proxy, including an immediate
latent-end proxy, and candidates that encounter any latent-time stop remain
joint-action structural endpoints but are outside the continuous local-Fisher
population. All candidates remain in the structural audit. For each family,
calibration must show at least 95% natural visible exit without a latent-time
generic stop and at least 80% `TRACE-STABLE` coverage. The strong P1 result must pass in the
trace-stable population and have consistent direction in the broader
same-injected-proxy finite-exit population. Different-proxy candidates are
reported only through prevalence, finite-step KL where defined, task utility,
and structural-failure endpoints; their joint action has no corresponding
`z`-only Fisher ranking. If the apparent effect exists only for changed
proxy/support/exit traces, the recursive Fisher claim is `KILL`.

### 2.4 Population and conditioning

The P1 population is hierarchical:

1. public problem group `g`;
2. frozen deterministic pre-action context `c`;
3. natural update family `f`;
4. construction trajectory or seed `k`;
5. independent Fisher histories `s_F`;
6. independent finite-step label histories `s_E`; and
7. independent direct-MC and utility histories.

Claims are conditional on the deterministic context distribution and averaged
over preregistered problem groups. Updates and histories nested under one
problem are not independent scientific units.

## 3. Visible continuation laws

### 3.1 Primary full-support analysis law

After deterministic latent closure, the primary observable policy is

\[
\widetilde\pi_z(a\mid h)
=\operatorname{softmax}(\ell_z(h)/0.6)_a
\]

over model probability rows `0..128255`, with `top_k=-1`, `top_p=1.0`, and the
frozen source token/string/length termination semantics. Let `T_term` be the
first visible decision that triggers any such termination; that emission counts
and every later state is absorbing. No row is added for tokenizer-only id
`128256`. This temperature-only law is a controlled,
full-support analysis distribution; it must never be described as the released
top-30/top-p deployment sampler.

For directional `KL(P_0 || P_v)`, sample each visible history from the reference
law after reference latent closure. Run candidate latent closure from the same
pre-action context, then teacher-force the candidate on exactly that reference
visible prefix. Independent candidate continuations do not estimate this KL.

The same implementation must perform sampling and density evaluation. The
directional score includes the temperature factor and differentiates through
all future deterministic latent mixtures and the complete candidate cache.

### 3.2 Official truncated-law sensitivity

The released visible configuration uses temperature `0.6`, top-p `0.95`, and
top-k `30`. A separate sensitivity implementation must reproduce the backend's
joint filtering order, sorting/tie convention, and renormalization. Returned
source `next_token_logprobs` are not accepted as density truth because the
audited source normalizes a different support in that path.

If any token with positive reference mass has zero candidate mass, the
conditional forward KL is `+infinity`. The implementation may not clamp it,
add epsilon, discard the history, or replace it with a full-softmax KL. Report:

- first support-loss time;
- cumulative top-k/top-p support churn;
- finite versus infinite path prevalence; and
- results on the subset whose visible support is stable, clearly labeled as a
  conditional sensitivity.

No expected-KL ratio is reported when its denominator or numerator is
extended-real. An effect that exists only under hard-support churn fails the
primary claim.

\[
\tau_{\rm sem}(y)=\min\{t:\operatorname{OnlineParser}(y_{\le t})=1\}

## 5. Natural update families

Every candidate is constructed without its Fisher or finite-step risk label.
Random directions and coordinate changes are excluded from the primary
population.

### 5.1 Family A: `LatentPolicy-ACTION`

At the common first-action context, use the exact source training sampler with
`add_noise_gumbel_softmax=True` to generate

\[
\bigl(z^A_{c,k},p^A_{c,k}\bigr)
=\operatorname{SourceNoiseAction}(\ell(c);\epsilon_k),
\qquad
v^A_{c,k}=z^A_{c,k}-z_0(c).
\]

`v^A` is a continuous primary direction only when `p^A` survives the source stop
check and equals the nonterminal reference proxy. A stopping proxy is recorded
under the `LATENT_FINISH_*` endpoint and no proposed embedding is consumed. If
the proxy does not stop the request and `p^A=524`, the source execution is the
hard one-hot exit transition `E_524`, not the proposed soft `z^A`; if another
non-stopping proxy changes, the joint action is a structural endpoint. Only the
proposed first action is noisy; after source execution, Section 2.2's
deterministic latent closure resumes with the same stop-first rule at every
later latent action.

Store the exact RNG state and all source-required full-noise or support data
needed to reproduce the action. Record raw logits, top-p eligibility set,
clamped/shifted Gumbel values, perturbed top-10 ids/scores, mixture weights,
action norm, proposed and executed proxy/action, nearest-reference distance,
entropy, and every later trace/exit diagnostic. Every construction seed is
retained; no reward- or risk-selected action search is permitted.

The unscaled `alpha=1` source action is primary. Same-support convex
interpolations may be reported as finite-step sensitivities but cannot replace a

### 5.2 Family B: `LatentPolicy-SOURCE-GRAD`

Construction histories disjoint from `s_F` and `s_E` must reproduce the pinned
source actor surrogate, including position alignment, stored selected ids and
perturbed scores, PPO ratio and clipping, advantage-dependent backward
convention, masks, loss aggregation, entropy configuration, and reward levels.
The primary construction is the frozen-checkpoint first-update case with
`J_source=-policy_loss`: old and current policies are identical, so the PPO
self-ratio must equal one to the frozen numerical tolerance before clipping. No
later-epoch or post-update ratio may be substituted. Bind the released branch:
GRPO group size eight, the exact include-overlong first-mask/winner advantage
routine and its max-length zeroing behavior, clip low/high `0.2`, dual clip

`algorithm.use_kl_in_reward=False`, entropy coefficient zero, and no actor KL

implementation and its straight-through sign convention; the fallback ordinary
token-logprob branch is a hard failure.

Let `J_source` be the source maximization objective, `ell` the first-action
logits, and `h` the **final RMSNorm output supplied directly to the checkpoint's
logit processor/head**. Let `L(h)` denote that exact pinned logit map. Define

\[
g^\ell_{c,k}=\nabla_\ell \widehat J^{\rm source}_{c,k},
\qquad
g^h_{c,k}=\left(\frac{\partial L(h)}{\partial h}\right)^\top
g^\ell_{c,k}.
\]

For the audited checkpoint, `L(h)=Wh`: the tied/frozen head is biasless and the
preflight must verify that logit scale and softcap are absent. If any scale,
softcap, bias, or alternative pre-norm state is used, its exact Jacobian belongs
inside `dL/dh`; the shortcut `W^T g^ell` is forbidden.

Apply a calibration-scaled **hidden-state counterfactual**, not a head or
parameter update:

\[
h^+_{c,k}=h(c)+\eta g^h_{c,k},
\quad
\ell^+_{c,k}=L(h^+_{c,k}),
\quad
v^B_{c,k}=z_{\rm det}(\ell^+_{c,k})-z_0(c).
\]

Set `p^B=argmax ell^+` and apply the same source execution map as Family A.
Only a non-stopping `p^B=p_0!=524` defines the continuous primary direction
above; stop-token/string/length outcomes and all other proxies remain absorbing
or branch structural endpoints in the complete construction audit. No `z^B` is
described as executed when the source stop check terminates the request.

This gives every candidate the same risk base `z_0`, avoids an unreplayable
same-Gumbel support counterfactual, and constrains the logit delta to the frozen
head's reachable column space. It is an objective-derived local activation
counterfactual. It is not an official optimizer step and cannot support a
parameter-update claim.

The 29 `AM-fit` groups choose `eta` from a frozen scale grid solely by closeness
to the Family-A median action norm, subject to the Section 2.3 exit/trace gates.
It may not be chosen to improve a horizon result. Mandatory controls are the source
surrogate without the backward-only sign flip, a selected-support ordinary
score, sign/reward shuffle, and the unprojected dense-logit counterfactual. If
only the sign-flipped or off-column-space variant produces the effect, Family B
fails.

Family B is primary only if the source objective is exactly replayable, at
least two reward/advantage levels occur at the frozen minimum prevalence, and
the step remains inside the construction and trace-support gates. An answer-NLL
gradient may not replace it after outcomes are seen.

## 6. Risk objects and estimators

### 6.1 Directional expected trajectory Fisher

For a trace-stable context and primary full-support law, let `ell_t(z,h)` be the
raw visible logits after recursively closing the deterministic latent process,
and define

\[
\bar\ell_t=\ell_t/0.6,
\qquad
\widetilde\pi_t=\operatorname{softmax}(\bar\ell_t),
\qquad
J_t=\frac{\partial\bar\ell_t}{\partial z}.
\]

The theoretical object is

\[
F_H(c)=\sum_{t=1}^{H}
\mathbb E_{y_{<t}\sim P_0}
\left[J_t^\top C(\widetilde\pi_t)J_t\right],
\qquad
q_H(v)=\tfrac12v^\top F_Hv,
\]

`J_t` therefore includes the temperature factor exactly once and derivatives
through all later latent mixtures and the visible cache. The implementation may
not divide by temperature again inside `C(pi)`. JVPs hold the reference
discrete top-10 supports, proxy sequence, and exit trace fixed and are valid only
inside the audited margin cell. Finite-difference tests remain inside that cell.
The emission step that triggers `T_term` contributes normally; every later
absorbing step contributes zero. P1 estimates only `q_H(v)` for preregistered natural updates;
it does not estimate or diagonalize the full activation-space matrix.

The default conditional categorical-JVP estimator is

\[
\widehat q_H^{\rm cat}(v)
=\frac{1}{2R_F}\sum_{r=1}^{R_F}\sum_{t=1}^{H}
\mathbf 1\{t\le T_r\}
\sum_a\widetilde\pi_{r,t}(a)
\left[D_v\log\widetilde\pi_{r,t}(a)\right]^2.
\]

Histories are streamed and accumulated in CPU float64. A sampled path-score
outer-product estimator may replace it only if it passes all family-specific
stability gates at lower measured wall time. A single rollout is always labeled
as a sample, never as the expected Fisher.

The semantic member is the same legal stopped-process object, not a score chosen
after reading the suffix:

\[
\widehat q_{\rm semantic}(v)
=\frac{1}{2R_F}\sum_{r=1}^{R_F}\sum_{t=1}^{H_{\rm cert}}
\mathbf 1\{t\le T_r\wedge\tau_{{\rm sem},r}\}
\sum_a\widetilde\pi_{r,t}(a)
\left[D_v\log\widetilde\pi_{r,t}(a)\right]^2.
\]

`tau_sem,r` is evaluated once from the reference history under the parser frozen
in Section 4.3 and is held fixed for the candidate density/JVP evaluation.

### 6.2 Finite-step behavioral labels

Using independent `s_E` histories, define

\[
d_{a:b}(v)=
\mathbb E_{y\sim P_0}
\left[\sum_{t=a}^{\min(b,T_{\rm term})}
\mathrm{KL}\!\left(
\widetilde\pi_0(\cdot\mid y_{<t})\Vert
\widetilde\pi_v(\cdot\mid y_{<t})
\right)\right].
\]

The primary label is **post-H8 tail risk** `d_{9:H_eval}`. It is not called a
tail beyond `H_cert`, because `H_cert` is larger than eight. Mandatory secondary
labels are `d_{1:H_eval}`, `d_{H_cert+1:H_eval}`, per-token curves, termination
changes, task reward, format validity, and response length.

The high-precision label and its confidence interval are an oracle/endpoint in
P1. No local quadratic result is called a finite-step certificate.

## 7. Mandatory predictors, baselines, and specificity controls

Every applicable calibration-selected member is retained:

1. `q_H` at `H in {1,2,3,8,semantic,H_cert}`;
2. H1 as the FishBack-style one-output pullback risk; FishBack is not double
   counted unless its complete steering/retuning method is implemented;
3. activation whitening, Euclidean norm, whitened norm, and cosine specifically
   to the source objective direction and Family-A mean direction;
4. fixed factual-path conditional Fisher/KL versus expected-history Fisher;
5. exact scalar and same-support convex retuning to a common H8 local budget,
   with arbitrary scaled edits labeled non-natural sensitivities;
6. smaller raw steps and matched raw action norms;
7. direct reference-rollout continuation-KL Monte Carlo on a risk-versus-time
   Pareto frontier, with equal wall time primary and equal generated/forced
   tokens secondary;
8. recurrent latent-Jacobian spectral radius, numerical abscissa,
   transient-growth, and STARS-style diagnostics;
9. the fully specified cross-checked adapted-metric tangent-growth competitor in
   [`P1_ADAPTED_METRIC_BASELINE_CONTRACT.md`](P1_ADAPTED_METRIC_BASELINE_CONTRACT.md),
   after independent review and freeze; a name-only analogue is forbidden;
10. latent top-10 churn, proxy/exit margin, visible support churn, decoder
    entropy, action density, nearest-update distance, and intervention OOD;
11. the long `H_eval` and `H_stab` oracles, never eligible as deployable
    methods;
12. the last nonterminal soft-latent interface under the same update family and
    visible law;
13. a `LATE-MATCHED-V` stress interface that transports the exact early vector
    to the last nonterminal latent base;
14. a norm- and layer-matched ordinary non-latent input-embedding interface; and
15. a latent-disabled or ordinary explicit-CoT control when source-compatible.

`best-simple` has one binding meaning. First define the core single-feature set

\[
\mathcal S_{\rm core}=\{q_1,q_2,q_3,q_8,q_{\rm semantic},
\lVert v\rVert_2,\lVert v\rVert_{W},q^{\rm fixed\ path}_8\}.
\]

`best-simple_core` is the calibration-selected winner between (i) the best
oriented single member of this exact set and (ii) a nonnegative ridge combiner
over the standardized eight features in the displayed order. Next form the
exact augmented stacking vector

\[
x_{\rm aug}=(s_{\rm core},-m_{\rm proxy},-m_{10/11},-m_{\rm exit},
c_{\rm latent},c_{\rm visible},s_{\rm recur},s_{\rm OOD}),
\]

where `s_core` is the nested out-of-fold core prediction on the 29 `AM-fit`
groups and their refit for test; the three `m` terms are frozen proxy, top-10
boundary, and latent-exit margins; `c_latent` is read only from the common full
latent trace/interpolation gate already charged to every screen; `c_visible` is
released-law support churn through H8 only and may not inspect `H_cert` or
`H_eval`; `s_recur` is the maximum oriented recurrent-stability diagnostic using
at most the first eight available post-intervention latent transition JVPs; and
`s_OOD` uses construction-time action density/nearest-update information with no
visible rollout or risk label. Every generated/forced token and derivative call
for these features is charged to `best-simple_aug`. That predictor is the
calibration-selected winner between every oriented component of `x_aug` and a
nonnegative ridge combiner over all eight components. The bare term
`best-simple` everywhere else in this protocol means **`best-simple_aug`**;
`best-simple_core` is an ablation only.

Both stacking levels use nested leave-one-problem-group-out cross-fitting.
Feature orientation, standardization, ridge lambda grid, candidate-order tie
rule, and winner rule are frozen before `GO-CAL`; the final selected model is
refit on all 29 `AM-fit` problems and hashed before any `AM-conformal` access.
Neither level may use fixed-horizon `q_Hcert` (the separately defined bounded semantic
stopping rule is allowed), `H_eval`, finite-step labels outside calibration, or
trace-failure postselection. The remaining declared cosine, density, entropy,
and stability members are still evaluated as individual mandatory controls even
when not selected into the binding combiner. Direct-MC and the adapted-metric
contract are not hidden inside `best-simple`; each remains a separately declared
IUT competitor.

The recursive-specificity interfaces are also unique. The natural late control
is the last nonterminal reference soft-latent action, fixed without outcome
search, with new candidates constructed by the same A/B extractor and stop-first
execution rules. `LATE-MATCHED-V` additionally injects `z_last,0+v` using each
early candidate's exact vector in the shared embedding coordinates while fixing
the already-emitted current proxy to the nonterminal late reference proxy. All
later mixtures, proxies, stops, exit, and cache are recursively recomputed. This
is a same-vector activation stress control, not a source-generated late update,
and it must pass the same interpolation/trace gate.

For the ordinary controls, hash before calibration a latent-disabled explicit-
CoT prompt transformation that preserves the problem statement and checkpoint.
Let `y_1^0` be its deterministic first visible response token. The
ordinary intervention is the shared embedding/residual-stream input
`E[y_1^0]` as it is fed to predict response token two; this has the same layer,
coordinate dimension, dtype, and first-generated-action position as the latent
mixture input. Horizon `t=1` for this control is the next visible decision.

Two ordinary banks are mandatory:

1. `AR-MATCHED-V` injects `E[y_1^0]+v` using each early latent candidate's exact
   vector and norm. It is a matched stress control, not a natural visible-policy
   update.
2. `AR-ACTION` uses fixed-seed samples `y_1^k` from the primary full-support
   visible law to form direction-derived counterfactuals
   `v_AR=E[y_1^k]-E[y_1^0]`. Construction-only deterministic norm-bin matching
   to the A/B banks is allowed and frozen before labels; no risk/reward rejection
   is allowed. This is called action-derived, not an executed soft visible token.

Each control uses its own identically defined `best-simple_aug`, full-support
expected-history Fisher, post-H8 label, and compute accounting. The explicit-CoT
template, token position, reference token, construction seeds, norm bins, and
failure/no-op rule are frozen before calibration.

If early, late, and either ordinary interface exhibit practically equivalent
Horizon Gaps, P1 downgrades to a generic autoregressive activation diagnosis.

## 8. Splits, RNG, and scientific units

### 8.1 Problem splits

Split stable near-duplicate problem groups before tokenization into:

- source/support preflight;
- calibration;
- held-out risk/decision test;
- optional larger utility-only test; and
- mechanism-distinct replication.

Within every calibration or test problem, generate its context and candidate
updates and then use disjoint RNG namespaces for construction, Fisher histories,
finite-step labels, direct MC, and utility. These are nested objects, not
mutually exclusive problem splits.

### 8.2 RNG and attrition

Freeze nonoverlapping namespaces for:

- action/update construction;
- reward/advantage construction;
- Fisher histories `s_F`;
- finite-step labels `s_E`;
- direct-MC baselines;
- utility histories; and
- bootstrap/resampling.

Seed equality is a hard error. Reference and candidate share one forced visible
prefix inside a directional KL sample, but construction and label histories do
not overlap.

Freeze the number of contexts per problem, candidate-order rule, no-op fallback,
and problem weighting. Each problem receives equal top-level weight regardless
of how many nested candidates remain eligible. Timeout and trace instability are
counted before any conditional subset analysis.

### 8.3 Bootstrap unit

The independent scientific unit is the problem group. First aggregate or
weight contexts and updates within a problem according to the frozen rule, then
resample problem groups. Nested `s_E` histories are resampled inside each
problem when label uncertainty is propagated. Updates and histories are never
treated as independent prompts.

Before calibration, run only source/adapter tests and a fixed microbenchmark at

- reference sample;
- candidate same-history force;
- deterministic recursive closure;
- input JVP/VJP through the latent suffix;
- full-vocabulary directional categorical score;
- source-objective backward for Family B;

after inspecting outcomes.

## 10. Calibration and power

### 10.1 Test sample size

Use exactly 48 calibration problem groups: 29 `AM-fit` and 19 untouched
`AM-conformal` groups as required by the adapted-metric contract. If source-only
eligibility or the frozen compute cap cannot supply them, P1 is `NO-GO`; the
conformal unit count is not repaired by treating nested candidates as groups.
All update-scale, history-count, threshold, feature, baseline, and power choices
use the 29 fit groups only. The 19 conformal groups are opened afterward solely
to form the frozen adapted-metric group-max residual quantile; they may not tune
any P1 or competitor choice.
For every primary family and endpoint, form one paired
problem-level effect `D_g`. Let `sigma_f^U` be the one-sided 90% upper bound on
its problem-level standard deviation from a frozen robust cluster-bootstrap
procedure. For the Spearman margin gate, target one-sided alpha `0.05` and power
`0.80`:

\[
n_{\rho,f}=\left\lceil
\left(
\frac{(z_{.95}+z_{.80})\sigma_f^U}{0.10-0.03}
\right)^2
\right\rceil.
\]

Use calibration-parametric, problem-level paired simulation for recall,
false-safe, expected-KL contrast, recursive-specificity, every mandatory-control
contrast, trace/exit coverage precision, and utility non-inferiority. Nested
candidates are never treated as independent Bernoulli trials. Freeze

\[
n_{\rm risk}=16\left\lceil
\frac{\max_f\{32,n_{\rho,f},n_{\rm recall,f},n_{\rm FS,f},n_{\rm contrast,f},
n_{\rm spec,f},n_{\rm controls,f},n_{\rm coverage,f}\}}{16}
\right\rceil.
\]

Risk-test `n>128` is `NO-GO`. Utility uses its own preregistered split and may be
larger because scoring the two selected candidates is cheaper; its cap must be
frozen after the microbenchmark. Binary 2% accuracy non-inferiority may not be
claimed from an underpowered 128-problem test.

The final power artifact freezes every variance-bound construction, simulation
seed, candidate tie rule, and the minimum rankable count of six trace-stable
same-proxy updates per context. A context below six follows the preregistered
failure/worst-case rule; it is never dropped. A problem with no rankable context
also remains in eligibility and power accounting.

### 10.2 Fisher and label history counts

Using only the 29 `AM-fit` groups, for each `R in {4,8,16,32}` use two
independent `R`-history replicates drawn from frozen nested pools. Select the
smallest `R_F` and `R_E` for which both families pass all applicable conditions,
then freeze those counts before opening any `AM-conformal` group:

1. at least 90% of calibration contexts have replicate risk-ranking Spearman
   at least `0.90`;
2. at least 90% have top-risk-set Jaccard at least `0.80`;
3. directional risks have frozen symmetric error
   `2|q1-q2|/(|q1|+|q2|+lambda_q)` below `0.15` in at least 90% of contexts;
4. finite-step label CI half-width is at most
   `max(0.15*tau_d, delta_abs)` in at least 90% of contexts; and
5. indeterminate finite-step safety labels are at most 10% per family.

`lambda_q` and `delta_abs` are fixed from numerical and practical margins, not
from held-out effects. Use the maximum required `R` across families. If no
`R<=32` passes, the low-compute P1 is `NO-GO`.

Choose the lowest-wall-time estimator that passes every stability gate; ties go
to the conditional categorical-JVP estimator. Delete all projected-eigenvalue
selection: P1 has no arbitrary projected matrix.

### 10.3 Other calibration-only choices

Before any `AM-conformal` access, the source/support preflight and 29 `AM-fit`
groups fix:

- Rule A or B;
- `m=8` or `12` under the frozen compute cap;
- Family-B `eta` by the norm/trace rule only;
- whitening floor and numerical damping;
- semantic parser;
- unsafe threshold `tau_d`;
- common target coverage `c_star`, exact rank/tie rule, and secondary
  predictor-specific fixed thresholds `kappa_j`;
- `best-simple_core`, `best-simple_aug`, every mandatory standalone competitor,
  and their nested cross-fitting/final-refit artifacts; and
- exact CI/bootstrap settings.

No choice may maximize a test Horizon Gap.

### 10.4 Oracle stability

On calibration-only independent history replicates, construct the `q` pair
from two disjoint `s_F` pools and the `d` pair from two disjoint `s_E` pools;
no pool is shared across objects. Aggregate at problem-group level and require:

- `Spearman(q_Hcert, q_Hstab)` lower confidence bound at least `0.95`;
- problem-cluster lower confidence bound for top-20% set Jaccard at least
  `0.80`;
- problem-cluster upper confidence bound for disagreement between the
  `H_cert` and `H_stab` screens at the same frozen coverage at most `0.05`; and
- `Spearman(d_9:Heval, d_9:Hstab)` lower confidence bound at least `0.95`, with
  problem-cluster lower confidence bound for top-20% Jaccard at least `0.80`.

If the longer oracle materially reorders candidates, `H_cert` is not a stable
fixed witness and P1 is `NO-GO`. `H_stab` is distinct from `H_eval`; no duplicate
`2*H_cert=H_eval` check is mislabeled as extra stability.

## 11. Primary analyses

### 11.1 Ranking and recall

Within each context/family, rank trace-stable natural updates by
`d_9:H_eval`. Compute Spearman and top-20% recall for each predictor. Aggregate
paired long-versus-best-simple margins at equal problem weight with a nested
problem-cluster bootstrap.

Each mandatory family must achieve:

- Spearman improvement at least `0.10`, one-sided 95% lower bound at least
  `0.03`; and
- top-20% recall improvement at least `0.10` with positive one-sided lower
  confidence bound.

Against each separately declared matched-time direct-MC, adapted-metric,
recurrent-stability, and other mandatory cheap control, `H_cert` must also have
a Spearman point margin at least `0.03` with one-sided lower confidence bound
above zero. Each contrast is its own IUT component. A control within 0.03 is
practically equivalent and closes the claimed advantage even if another weaker
control loses.

Report the same estimates on the broader same-injected-proxy finite-exit
population. Different-proxy candidates have no `z`-only local-Fisher ranking;
report their prevalence and finite behavioral/utility endpoints separately.
Disagreement caused by trace changes is not credited to the local-horizon
mechanism.

### 11.2 Simultaneously confirmed false-safe decisions

Finite-step label states are:

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{confirmed unsafe}&:\operatorname{LCB}_{\rm maxT}(d)>\tau_d,\\
\text{confirmed safe}&:\operatorname{UCB}_{\rm maxT}(d)\le\tau_d,\\
\text{indeterminate}&:\operatorname{LCB}_{\rm maxT}(d)\le\tau_d<
\operatorname{UCB}_{\rm maxT}(d).
\end{aligned}
\]

The one-sided max-T bounds are simultaneous over every candidate in one
family/test and are recomputed from shared-history bootstrap replicates. A
pointwise 95% interval cannot support the word “confirmed.”
`UCB(d)>tau_d` alone never proves an update unsafe.

Calibration freezes target problem-weighted acceptance coverage
`c_star in {0.20,0.30,...,0.80}` without test labels. On test, predictor `j`
accepts its lowest-risk scored candidates under the frozen rank/tie rule until
it reaches exactly `c_star`; therefore the primary comparison has common
coverage by construction. If the shared structural gate leaves insufficient
eligible mass, the family is `NO-GO`, not evaluated at a smaller favorable
coverage. Fixed `kappa_j` results and coverage-equivalence confidence intervals
are secondary.

Before either predictor acts, apply the identical calibration-frozen structural
gate to every candidate. Different-proxy, timeout, and declared trace-OOD
candidates are rejected by both screens and retained in coverage/accounting;
the gate's recursive/interpolation cost is charged to both. The ranking endpoint
uses the trace-stable population, while the operational false-safe denominator
includes all constructed candidates after this common rejection rule. A method
cannot win by owning a stricter structural gate.

Problem-weighted false-safe prevalence is

\[
p_j=\frac1G\sum_g\frac1{m_g}\sum_{i\in g}
\mathbf1\{A_{jgi}=1,\operatorname{LCB}_{\rm maxT}(d_{gi})>\tau_d\}.
\]

Also report common-coverage selective risk `r_j=p_j/c_star`. Indeterminate
labels are governed by a binding partial-identification contrast, not merely a
sensitivity table. For one globally consistent assignment
`a_gi in {0,1}` of every indeterminate candidate to safe/unsafe, let `p_j(a)` be
the same prevalence with `a_gi` substituted for the unobserved unsafe status and
define

\[
\Delta_{\rm WC}=\max_{a\in\{0,1\}^{|I|}}
\{p_{\rm long}(a)-p_{\rm simple}(a)\}.
\]

The maximizing assignment counts a long-only accepted indeterminate as unsafe
and a simple-only accepted indeterminate as safe; a candidate accepted by both
screens receives one common assignment and cancels from the contrast. This is
the most adverse admissible assignment for the long screen. Report each
screen's all-indeterminate-unsafe bound as a secondary quantity.

The long screen must satisfy all of:

- best-simple false-safe prevalence at least 10%;
- at least eight confirmed false-safe updates across at least eight problem
  groups and at least 20% problem coverage;
- point reduction at least 5 percentage points and 25% relative;
- paired nested-bootstrap upper confidence bound for
  `p_long-p_simple` below `-0.03`;
- paired upper confidence bound for `r_long-r_simple` below zero;
- paired nested-bootstrap upper confidence bound for `Delta_WC` below zero;
- relative-risk upper bound at most `0.75` if the relative claim is made;
- overall rejection rate at most 80%, equivalently acceptance coverage at
  least 20%; and
- indeterminate prevalence at most 10%.

The bootstrap resamples problem groups and, inside each replicate, `s_F`,
direct-MC, and `s_E` histories. Predictor scores, max-T label bounds, common-
coverage decisions, ordinary and worst-case assignments, and simple/long
contrasts are recomputed together with calibration-frozen thresholds and rules.
Thus the claim covers repeated estimation, not merely the realized predictor
scores.

### 11.3 Matched-utility consequence

Freeze one construction-only utility

\[
u^C_{gi}=\operatorname{mean}\{\text{pinned source scalar reward on the
construction RNG pool}\},
\]

including the exact reward components, normalization, rollout count, and code
hash. It contains no `s_E` or held-out utility reward. For each screen
`j in {simple,long}` separately, choose the candidate with maximum `u^C` from
that screen's own accepted set. If that set is empty, use no-op `z_0`; ties
follow frozen construction order. “Accepted by either screen” is not a valid
shared selector.

Evaluate the two selected candidates with independent histories. Primary task
utility is exact-answer/source reward under the exact released visible
top-k/top-p evaluation law, for which reward is well-defined even when
directional KL is infinite. Full-support task utility and truncated-law support
failure are mandatory sensitivities; no law is substituted after outcomes.

Let `D_j,g` be the frozen equal-context aggregate of full-support
`d_9:H_eval` for the candidate selected by screen `j`. The descriptive risk
ratio is the ratio of problem-level means

\[
R_d=\frac{G^{-1}\sum_gD_{L,g}}{G^{-1}\sum_gD_{S,g}},
\]

not a mean of per-instance ratios. Calibration freezes a positive denominator
floor `delta_0`; require `LCB(mu_S)>delta_0`, where `mu_j=mean_g D_j,g`.

Inference uses paired linear contrasts

\[
C_r=\mu_L-r\mu_S.
\]

Require point `R_d<=0.90` and one-sided 95% `UCB(C_1)<0`. Wording of “at least
10% reduction” additionally requires `UCB(C_0.9)<0`. Report a Fieller or paired-
bootstrap ratio interval only as a secondary summary; no decision depends on a
single unlucky bootstrap denominator.

For utility difference `Delta_U=U_L-U_S`, require the paired one-sided 95% lower
bound above `-delta_NI`, with `delta_NI<=0.02` normalized reward or two absolute
accuracy points. A point estimate alone is not non-inferiority.

The consequence bootstrap is paired at problem level and resamples `s_F`, every
screen-specific recurrent/adapted JVP history, direct-MC histories,
construction-utility histories, `s_E`, and held-out utility histories inside
each problem. It first rebuilds every applicable screen score and accepted set,
then recomputes each screen's `u^C` selector, finite-step-risk contrast, and
utility contrast in the same replicate. Thus selector uncertainty includes
screen-score randomness rather than conditioning on the realized accepted set.
The candidate action bank itself is the preregistered intervention population
and remains fixed.

### 11.4 Recursive specificity

Define each interface's Horizon Gap as its long-versus-`best-simple_aug` paired
Spearman margin under the unique interfaces in Section 7. The early interface
must exceed the natural last-latent, `LATE-MATCHED-V`, `AR-MATCHED-V`, and
`AR-ACTION` controls separately by at least `0.05`, with a one-sided
problem-cluster lower confidence bound above zero for every contrast. At the
early interface, `H_cert` must still meet the
Section 11.1 Spearman point margin `0.10`, lower bound `0.03`, recall margin
`0.10`, every separate direct-MC/adapted-metric/stability IUT comparison, and
every Section 11.2 common-coverage false-safe gate including `Delta_WC`.
“Retains the direction” without these numeric thresholds is not a pass.

Failure of this section is a `DOWNGRADE` to generic activation diagnosis unless
another P0 kill rule applies.

### 11.5 Multiplicity

The strong claim is an intersection-union test: every mandatory component uses
one-sided `alpha=0.05`, and the claim passes only if every family and every
mandatory endpoint rejects its own failure null. No Bonferroni correction is
needed across components whose conjunction defines success. Any claim of “at
least one family/platform succeeds” is a union claim and uses Holm or a frozen
max-T procedure. Secondary outcomes are exploratory.

## 12. Binding pass, kill, and downgrade rules

### 12.1 P1 pass

The strong preferred-platform P1 passes only if both explicitly preregistered
primary families, `LatentPolicy-ACTION` and `LatentPolicy-SOURCE-GRAD`,
separately satisfy Sections 11.1-11.4 on untouched test data and:

1. source identity, token, recursive replay, and visible-law gates pass;
2. exit and trace-stable coverage pass;
3. oracle stability passes;
4. scalar/smaller-step, support, stability, direct-MC, and adapted-metric
   controls do not close the practical gap;
5. no delimiter, EOS, prompt, reward sign, context seed, support churn, or
   discrete branch explains the effect;
6. construction, Fisher, labels, MC, and utility histories remain disjoint; and
7. all declared IUT components pass.

A preferred-platform pass authorizes P2 or replication work. It does not by
itself support mechanism-general wording. That requires a qualified CODI,
SWITCH, or another materially distinct recurrence plus the later P0 claim
ladder.

### 12.2 Permanent or strong-project kill

Set the strong recursive-method project to `KILL` if any frozen P0 condition or
any of the following occurs:

1. only a last-action or clamped-exit estimand is implementable;
2. low-compute checkpoints lack four later latent actions or adequate visible
   semantic tails;
3. recursive candidate closure, joint proxy/action semantics, or same-history
   forcing cannot be reproduced;
4. full-support expected-history evaluation removes the apparent gap;
5. the effect is driven only by latent/visible support churn, timeout, proxy, or
   exit discontinuity;
6. H2/H3/H8, semantic prefix, whitening, smaller steps, direct MC, stability,
   or the adapted-metric baseline comes within the 0.03 practical-equivalence
   margin;
7. the effect survives only random, searched, sign-flipped, off-column-space,
   or intervention-OOD updates;
8. the objective-derived family fails or A/B collapse to one effective family;
   any optimizer/parameter replacement requires a new frozen protocol and
   untouched groups rather than rescuing this test;
9. `R>32`, risk `n>128`, or the frozen compute cap is required;
10. utility non-inferiority is underpowered at the maximum utility cap;
11. oracle ranking is unstable at `H_stab` or semantic completion;
12. direct continuation-KL MC is no more expensive and at least as accurate;
13. strong effects appear in fewer than two natural update families; or
14. the eventual P2 method reduces to always computing a longer Fisher and
    applying a standard natural gradient.

Use `DOWNGRADE` only when a preregistered diagnostic remains scientifically
valid but recursive specificity, operational effect, or cross-family generality
fails. Thresholds are not weakened after outcomes.
